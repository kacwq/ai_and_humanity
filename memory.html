What is Semantic Video Search?

A search engine that understands what you mean, not just what you type. It finds the exact moment in hours of video, audio, or text where your question is answered.

Every answer,
instantly findable.

Instead of scrolling through hours of video, type a question and get direct results — timestamped clips, book pages, and slides — all from [Name]'s content library.

It searches meaning, not just matching words. Ask it like you'd ask a person.

  • Searches podcast, book, and video library simultaneously
  • Semantic search — understands what you mean, not just what you typed
  • Results link directly to the relevant timestamp or page
  • Works in natural language — no keywords needed
  1. All video transcripts, book text, and slide content are processed and stored as searchable semantic embeddings.
  2. When you search, your query is compared against those embeddings to find the closest matches by meaning.
  3. Results are ranked by relevance and returned with links to exact timestamps or page numbers.
Why this matters

The bottleneck in learning has never been the availability of knowledge. It's the ability to find the right piece at the right moment. Semantic search removes that bottleneck — and what remains is pure curiosity.
